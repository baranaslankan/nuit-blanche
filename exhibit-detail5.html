The Project
Two Voices captures the sunrise and sunset, memories and moments. It forms an over a year period. One day per minute. 365 minutes corresponding to one year.

Two scenes facing each other. One facing East, the other facing West. Although at each dawn or dusk, the two images are the passage of time, the concerted code of the photo. The path of this transition is the combination, a leap second that connects itself. Additionally, it goes through the entire year. This connection keeps pace with anticipation and the gap. Sometimes the time will be lost in the night, or evening, the interval of the change, or even the two, the morning and the sunset, that occur in unison. When the viewer faces the two scenes, the sunrise or sunset, the memory simultaneously, the humor of action, the memory is reshaped, in layers of action, time and the other, as the moment goes and the moment relives.
Artist
Angela Bonetica and Rafael Lain were born in Caxias do Sul, Brazil. They live and work in Paris and São Paulo. They are renowned in the art world as multi-disciplinary artists, have exhibited at Biennials in São Paulo, Rome, Paris, Madrid, and the Americas, including the São Paulo Biennial, Havana, Cuba and the 27th Biennial in São Paulo, as well as many others.
