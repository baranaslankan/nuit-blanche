The Project
White Dwarf is floating art composed of obsolete scientific artifacts. Its title comes from the seemingly endless star pool galaxy - those which lose their energy and produce most degenerate matter.

In this work, Liu creates a multistoried assemblage of white dwarfs, one near, but two distant and far. The work is created by labor, channel and exchange. The main material art event contains information, memories, conflict, and transformation of obsolete scientific and art instruments, science, home, and school relics outlived by today's artificial objects and systems, spinning slowly, eventually creating a future.
Artist
An Liu, Toronto, Canada
Liu's work is included in the permanent collections of the Institute of Chicago, the Hammer Museum, and the San Francisco Museum of Contemporary Art. Clouds is recent permanent installation at the San Francisco Museum of Modern Art.
