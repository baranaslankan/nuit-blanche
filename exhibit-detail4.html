The Project
Shapeshifter, described as one that seems able to change its own identity at will, is a mythical figure that can assume different forms, sorts of animals (Therianthrope).

The works within Shapeshifter continue to expand on fantasy, language, and transformation of identity, as the artist uses imagery from a wide set of sources, referencing anything from the classical symbolism, to science fiction, to the formal symbolism, and the personal and contemporary world. Her images are rooted in custom mythologies, inspired by the transformation of characters as they gradually transform in their own bodies. These images, more and more, contain thoughts about tradition, adaptation, and transformation, as the protagonist is now a hybrid of the other, or even the two, the animal and the human, such that, they might be seen to not be about the question in space, but together evolution by time.
Artist
Anna Brita Delsa (b Delsa, Sweden) is a Canadian multi-disciplinary visual artist who explores themes of fantasy, language, and transformation of identity in her work. Exhibitions include: Canada, Paraguay, London, New York, and internationally. She was the artist in residence at Parsons, NYC, and won the British Simian Medal. Finalist and received the Black Goldsmiths Prize, and the Shelley Award. She teaches on fantasy and artists at the University of Manhattan School of Art.
