The Project
A young woman embarks on a journey into a dreamlike otherworld to discover that every tiny action we do in this world, has a direct resonance in a myriad of other realms. This film is part of the ÁRRAN 360° film project.

ÁRRAN 360° is a first-of-its-kind film project that will feature six original 360-degree screen-based works created by six leading Sámi artists from across Sápmi. ÁRRAN 360° was produced concurrently with the ARCTIC XR project, which features 360° video works by Circumpolar Indigenous artists located across Canada. These works will be shown in conjunction for Nuit Blanche Toronto and projected onto an immersive circular display. This film is part of the ÁRRAN 360° film project.
Artist
Ann Holmgren, Sápmi, Sweden
Ann Holmgren is the daughter of a reindeer herding mother and a Swedish father from northern Sweden, Sápmi. She is a graduate of the Norwegian Film School and is currently based in Norway.
